MMI Daily Iron Ore Index Report December 18 2025

Hellenic Shipping News  |  18 December 2025  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/mmi-daily-iron-ore-index-report-december-18-2025

inChinese iron ore and steelmaking prices18/12/2025

The main iron ore contract, I2605, trended strongly, closing at 777.5 CNY/tonne, up 1.63% from the previous trading day.Traders were active in offloading cargoes, whilst steel mills remained cautious, maintaining a wait-and-see approach and procuring strictly on a needs basis. Overall trading sentiment was moderate. Port spot prices rose by 5–10 CNY/tonne compared to the previous trading day. According to SMM survey data, total inventory across the ten major ports currently stands at 110.26 million tonnes, an increase of 730,000 tonnes period-on-period. The inventory structure remains dominated by lump and concentrates; however, there are structural divergences within fines: medium-grade fines, such as PB Fines, are showing a clear destocking trend, whilst stocks of Mac Fines and Newman Fines remain at low levels. Conversely, Jinbuba Fines continue to accumulate. Total inventory of the five major steel products is also currently at a periodic low. Coupled with recent environmental production restrictions in regions such as Hebei and Sichuan, as well as maintenance overhauls at certain steel mills, production output has declined. This is conducive to alleviating inventory pressure at steel mills. Furthermore, generally positive macroeconomic news, combined with a significant surge in coking coal prices, has boosted market sentiment. Consequently, iron ore tracked other commodities within the ferrous complex to stage a slight rebound. However, considering the widening gap between supply and demand is capping price gains, the upside potential for iron ore is expected to be limited, with a risk of a correction in the short term.
