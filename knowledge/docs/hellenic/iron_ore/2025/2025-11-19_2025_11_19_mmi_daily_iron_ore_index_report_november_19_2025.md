in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 19/11/2025

## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 19/11/2025
Today, DCE iron ore continued to hold up well, with the most-traded contract I2601 closing at 791.5 yuan, up 0.76% from theprevious trading day. Traders sold at market prices, while steel mills showed limited inquiries and procured at lower prices. Overall market sentiment was moderate. In Shandong, PB fines traded at 785-795 yuan, basically flat from yesterday's transaction price; in Hebei, 60.8% PB fines dominated transactions at 795-800 yuan/mt, also basically flat from the previous day. According to the SMM survey, as of November 19, the blast furnace operating rate across 242 steel mills tracked by SMM stood at 86.60%, up 0.04 percentage point MoM. Daily average hot metal production at the sampled mills was 2.3957 million mt, down 2,000 mt WoW. Recently, maintenance plans for blast furnaces have been delayed, resulting in a relatively small decline in hot metal output. Iron ore demand continues to provide strong support for ore prices. However, considering that iron ore supply is still growing and inventory pressure is gradually increasing, upward room in the short term is limited, and prices may maintain a fluctuating trend within a narrow range.
