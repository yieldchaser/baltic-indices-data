## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 22/09/2025
Iron ore futures held up well today, with intraday performance slightly weakening. The most-traded contract I2601 closed at 808.5,up 0.37% from the previous trading day. Traders showed moderate willingness to sell; steel mills exhibited increased wait-and-see senment, with purchase willingness remaining moderate on Monday. Market transac on atmosphere was moderate. In Shandong, mainstream transac on prices for old PB fines were around 800 yuan/mt, up 0-5 yuan/mt from yesterday; new PB fines traded around 788-790 yuan/mt. In Tangshan, transac on prices for PB fines were 805-810 yuan/mt, flat from last Friday. Last week, SMM global iron ore shipments totaled 37.66 million mt, up 3.92 million mt WoW, an increase of 11.6%. Shipments from Australia and Brazil rebounded significantly, while those from non-mainstream countries such as South Africa, India, and Peru declined. SMM China iron ore arrivals totaled 23.96 million mt, down 2.08 million mt WoW, a decrease of approximately 8%. Short-term supply pressure remains rela vely limited. This week marks the final stage of concentrated restocking by steel mills, with demand con nuing to support ore prices. Addi onally, strong market expecta ons for subsequent policy announcements further reinforce ore price resilience. Overall, iron ore prices are expected to maintain a strong, rangebound fluctua ng trend in the short term.
