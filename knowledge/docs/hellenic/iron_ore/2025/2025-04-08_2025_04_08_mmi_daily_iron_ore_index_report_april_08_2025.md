## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 08/04/2025
Today, The DCE iron ore futures continued to fall sharply today. The most-traded contract rolled over to I2509, closing at 693, with a full-daydecline of 3.48%. Traders showed weak willingness to sell; steel mills were cautious and had moderate purchase willingness; market transaction sentiment was poor. In Shandong, the mainstream transaction price for PB fines was around 745 yuan/mt, down 20 yuan/mt from yesterday; in Tangshan, the transaction price for PB fines was around 760 yuan/mt, down 18-20 yuan/mt from yesterday. Today, the Sino-US tariff trade war escalated, leading to pessimistic market sentiment. Although the short-term impact of the trade war on iron ore was limited, market expectations and risk aversion weighed on prices. Additionally, according to SMM's weekly maintenance impact data, the impact this week decreased by 98,400 mt MoM. However, next week's impact is expected to increase by 900 mt MoM, a significant decrease. The growth in pig iron production narrowed, weakening fundamental support. Under the dual influence, iron ore futures prices continued to fall sharply. Aftention should be paid to future domestic stimulus policies.
