## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 14/11/2025
DCE iron ore futures continued to fluctuate rangebound with a stronger bias today, with the I2601 closingat 772.5 RMB, up 0.26% slightly from the previous trading day. Approaching the weekend, market transactions were sluggish. Spot cargo prices at ports remained largely stable compared to the previous trading day. In Shandong, transaction prices for PB fines with over 61% specifications were in the range of 780-785 RMB, up 0-2 RMB/mt from yesterday; in Hebei, mainstream transaction prices for 60.8% PB fines were between 785-788 RMB/mt, basically flat from yesterday. Since the start of winter, weather-related disruptions have increased, and rain and snow are expected next week. In addition, environmental protection-driven production restrictions have imposed certain constraints on transportation. To ensure production, some steel mills moderately increased their in-factory inventories, leading to a slight rise in port pick-up volume this week, which provided support for ore prices. However, looking ahead to next week, increased blast furnace maintenance is expected to result in a significant decline in hot metal production. Ore prices are projected to fluctuate rangebound under pressure in the coming week.
