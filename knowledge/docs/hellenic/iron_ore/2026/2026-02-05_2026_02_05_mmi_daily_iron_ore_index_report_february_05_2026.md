## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 05/02/2026
Today, the DCE iron ore market trended weaker. The benchmark contract, I2605, closed at 768.5 RMB/tonne,registering a decline of 1.73% from the previous trading session. Spot prices in the physical market dropped by 7-8 RMB/tonne compared to the prior day. Traders showed moderate enthusiasm for quo ng, while steel mills were cauous with inquiries, largely adopng a ‘wait-and-see' stance. Consequently, the overall market trading atmosphere remained average. Regarding fundamental data, SMM sta s cs for this week show inventory across 10 major ports at 117.8 million tonnes, a slight week-on-week decrease of 270,000 tonnes. Structurally, all four mainstream varie es exhibited a synchronous destocking trend. However, despite this marginal improvement in inventory levels, a review of the macro supply landscape reveals that global iron ore shipment volumes and arrivals at Chinese ports connue to run at high levels. With no clear signal of a decline in the short term, supply-side pressure remains significant. Looking ahead, although the recent slight rebound in daily average hot metal output indicates that rigid demand persists, the market is influenced by the approaching Lunar New Year holiday window. Concentrated pre-holiday restocking by steel mills has essen ally concluded. Consequently, subsequent demand for raw material procurement will inevitably enter a channel of marginal contrac on, and the rigid support underlying prices is gradually weakening. On balance, against a backdrop of sustained loose supply and weakening demand, iron ore prices are projected to operate under pressure in the short term, con nuing a vola le and weak trend.
