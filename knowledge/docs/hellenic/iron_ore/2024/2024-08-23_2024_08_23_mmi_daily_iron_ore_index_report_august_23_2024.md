## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 23/08/2024
Today, iron ore prices ran weak, accelerating their decline by the end of the session. The most-traded contract I2501 surged significantly, finallyclosing at 719.5 yuan/mt, with a daily drop of 2.24%. Traders' enthusiasm for selling weakened. Steel mills restocked as needed. Overall market transaction atmosphere was average today. In Shandong, PB fines traded at 720 yuan/mt, down 5-10 yuan/mt from yesterday; in Tangshan, PB fines traded at 735-740 yuan/mt, down 5-10 yuan/mt from yesterday. As of August 23, total inventory at the 35 ports tracked by SMM was 147.13 million mt, down 30,000 mt MoM, up 30.02 million mt YoY. Daily average shipments from ports of imported ore were 2.899 million mt, down 187,000 mt MoM. This week, port congestion increased. On the supply side, steel mills' profits remained under pressure, coupled with the recent rebound in iron ore prices, leading steel mills to mainly restock as needed. Overall, iron ore demand remained weak, and iron ore prices fluctuated at low levels under pressure.
