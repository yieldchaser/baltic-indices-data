## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 20/12/2024
Today, iron ore futures continued to drop slightly, with the most-traded I2505 contract closing at 769 yuan/mt, down 0.77% for the day.Traders showed low enthusiasm for selling, while steel mills remained cautious and exhibited average purchase willingness. Market transactions were sluggish today. In Shandong, mainstream transaction prices for PB fines were at 765-770 yuan/mt, down 5 yuan/mt from yesterday. In Tangshan, PB fines transaction prices were around 780 yuan/mt, also down 5 yuan/mt from yesterday. As of December 20, total inventory at 35 ports monitored by SMM stood at 145.85 million mt, down 810,000 mt WoW but up 30.23 million mt YoY. The daily average port pick-up volume of imported ore was 3.132 million mt, down 109,000 mt MoM but up 670,000 mt YoY. Affected by annual maintenance at some steel mills and environmental protection-driven production restrictions, pig iron production declined significantly. Coupled with falling ore prices, steel mills' purchase willingness weakened, leading to a notable drop in port pick-up volume. Looking ahead to next week, the fundamentals of iron ore are expected to remain weak, with ore prices likely to continue fluctuating downward.
