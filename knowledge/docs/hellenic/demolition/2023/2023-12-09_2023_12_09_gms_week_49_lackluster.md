## Main
in Weekly Demolition Reports 09/12/2023
Image reference: 2023-12-09_gms-week-49-lackluster_img1.jpg
The lackluster end to the year in sub continent recycling markets continues for another week as disappointed owners and cash buyers face selling their inventory into markets where prices have slumped.
Indeed, several dry bulk sales were done below USD 500/LT LDT into India in the lowest confirmed deals for several months.
Steel prices have declined in India by almost 10 percent, in a shock to many, particularly as international steel prices have pushed on to an impressive extent over the last month.
As such the offering and buying has been extremely tentative of late, with end buyers preferring to wait and watch market developments before offering firm on tonnage again.
Bangladesh and Pakistan meanwhile continue to face LC / financing difficulties and minimal deals have been confirmed to those markets as a result.
Chittagong may stay inert right up until the elections in mid January next year, with many hoping a change in regime brings some stimulus to the local economy and international exports in particular.
It is therefore encouraging to see dry bulk charter markets showing signs of life, and this is subsequently depriving recycling locations of tonnage at a time of considerable malaise there.
In other news, the UAE issued a new law yesterday which will come into force in March, 2024 and limit any UAE flagged or any foreign flagged vessels coming from UAE for HKC only recycling in the sub continent - along with some contradictory wording prohibiting ‘beaching'. Further clarifications are needed however to understand the full extend of the restrictions.
