## Summary
The tanker market is facing significant disruption as the continued closure of the Strait of Hormuz forces a record migration of VLCCs into the Atlantic, creating an oversupply that is weighing heavily on Western freight rates. Meanwhile, oil prices are projected to remain near $100 per barrel for the rest of 2026 due to tightening fundamentals and anticipated inventory drawdowns. Despite current positive sentiment, a rising vessel orderbook suggests a potential long-term market downcycle as new supply eventually begins to outpace global demand.
